MUTUAL FUND RECOMMENDATION SYSTEM — METHODOLOGY

HOW WE RECOMMEND FUNDS:
We fetch real NAV data from AMFI for the last 3 years.
We calculate 3 metrics for every fund:

1. CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate):
CAGR tells you how much a fund grew per year on average.
Formula: (End NAV / Start NAV) ^ (1/3) - 1
Example: If a fund grew from NAV 100 to 200 in 3 years, CAGR = 26%
Higher CAGR means better growth historically.

2. Volatility:
Volatility tells you how much the fund value went up and down.
Formula: Standard deviation of daily returns x square root of 252
Lower volatility means the fund was more stable.
Higher volatility means the fund value fluctuated a lot.

3. Sharpe Ratio:
Sharpe ratio tells you how much return the fund gave per unit of risk taken.
Formula: (CAGR - Risk Free Rate) / Volatility
We use 6.5% as risk free rate (RBI repo rate proxy).
Higher Sharpe ratio means better return for the risk taken.
A Sharpe ratio above 1 is generally considered good.

WHY WE RANK BY SHARPE RATIO:
Sharpe ratio is the best single metric because it balances both return and risk.
A fund with 20% CAGR but very high volatility may score lower than
a fund with 12% CAGR but very low volatility.
This protects investors from chasing high returns without understanding the risk.

HOW RISK LEVELS WORK:
Low Risk — Large Cap, Liquid, Debt funds
Medium Risk — Flexi Cap, Hybrid, ELSS funds
High Risk — Mid Cap, Small Cap funds

WHY ONLY THESE CATEGORIES:
Large Cap and Liquid funds have historically shown lower volatility.
Mid Cap and Small Cap funds have historically shown higher volatility but higher returns.
This is based on SEBI fund categorization guidelines.

HOW AGE AFFECTS RECOMMENDATIONS:
Age does not change which funds are recommended.
Age only helps explain the recommendation context.
Younger investors have more time to recover from market fluctuations.
Older investors may prefer stability over high growth.
